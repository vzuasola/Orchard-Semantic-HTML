WHAT DOES COOKING MEAN?

is it simply applying heat to a food product? A way of making certain food safe to eat? Or a way to create flavour and make food more appealing? This is just part of what Herve This, the father of molecular gastronomy, has dedicated his life to finding out. We spoke to him to find out what his experiments have told him. And in the process even discovered the secrets to cooking the perfect egg...
Keep water between 67 and 67°C for a flavourful, tender yolk

TASTE OF THE COLOURS

Red

Red foods remind us of berries and soft, so we anticipate a sweet test.

Green

Fresh, zingy green colours are reminiscent of unripe fruit, promosing sour or acid flavours.

White

White foods evoke memories of salt and salty flavours, driving the expectation of a savoury treat.
